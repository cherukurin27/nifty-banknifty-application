Why Option 1 (15-min Confirm) Fails on This Data

  • The 15-min Supertrend lags ~15–30 minutes behind actual trend changes. By the time 15-min ST flips GREEN, the 5-min entry has often already fired 3–6 candles earlier — and those early candles are the best entries.
  • In a fast intraday move (common in BankNifty), the 5-min ST can flip and run 200+ pts while the 15-min ST is still catching up. The filter blocks these exact high-quality trades.
  • The claim "most losing trades happen because 5-min briefly trends against the larger trend" is not supported by this data. The 5-min losing trades are NOT mostly counter-trend to the 15-min — the data shows NIFTY WR drops 74% → 63% with the filter, meaning it's blocking winners, not just losers.
  • The only scenario it helps: BankNifty 10–20d WR improves by +0.9–1.2pp. That's statistically noise on a sample of 13–18 trades.

Why Option 2 (ATR Cap) Works for BankNifty

  • BankNifty's ATR on a normal day ranges from 60 to 180 pts. The fixed 130pt cap was perfect on average but too tight on high-ATR days (ATR=100→ 2×ATR=200 vs fixed 130).
  • 2×ATR gave the trade room to breathe on volatile days without getting knocked out by noise, then the Supertrend trail locked in the profit.
  • Why it hurts avg loss slightly (−123 → −141 on 90d): on high-ATR days the cap is wider, so when a loss does occur it's larger. But the WR improvement (+8.5pp) more than compensates — net +1,435 pts over 90 days.
  • For Nifty, ATR is typically 20–45 pts. 2×ATR = 40–90 pts. The fixed 55pt cap was already sitting right in the middle of this range — hence no improvement.

Final Recommendation

DO NOT IMPLEMENT
Option 1 — 15-min Trend Confirm
The theory sounds right but the data says otherwise. It costs more in blocked winners than it saves in filtered losses. The strategy already has 5 filters doing trend confirmation — adding a 6th from a higher timeframe adds complexity for negative return.
