UI Enhancement Deep Analysis

Every screen, viewed as a working trader sitting at 9:30 AM — what's missing, what's redundant, what genuinely helps.

TAB 1 — Live Signals

Current state: Signal card + filter pills + chart. Good foundation. The gaps are all about context: you can see the numbers but can't quickly read the market temperature or know "is this a good moment to trade at all today?"

A. Strategy Pulse Bar — top of the page, one line, always visible

HIGHLIVE Market Regime Indicator (both symbols, one line)
Right now the page header just shows strategy parameters — static text. You open the app and don't know whether ADX is currently above threshold on either symbol, or if the market is in a dead zone.
Add a one-line "pulse bar" under the header: for each symbol show a tiny coloured pill — 🟢 Trending / 🟡 Borderline / 🔴 Sideways — derived from the latest ADX value. Trader sees at a glance whether the market is tradeable before reading the signal card.
HIGHLIVE Price vs Previous Day High/Low context
The indicator data already includes first15_high and the indicators module has prev_day_levels(). Knowing whether current price is above/below PDH or PDL is critical context for intraday entries — a BUY at PDH resistance is very different from a BUY after PDH breakout.
Add two small metric boxes to the signal card: PDH and PDL with a coloured indicator (price above/below). Also show ORB High (first15_high) since ORB filter is enabled — currently invisible to the trader.
HIGHLIVE ST Confirmation Progress counter
NIFTY requires 4 consecutive ST candles; BNKN requires 2. Right now if confirmation is incomplete, you see "❌ ST GREEN" with no idea how many candles into the streak you are. You might be 3/4 of the way there — a very different situation from 0/4.
Show "ST streak: 2/4" next to the ST filter pill when confirmation is in progress. Signals to the trader "almost there, next candle may fire".
MEDLIVE "Filters passed / 5" progress counter on the waiting card
When waiting for a signal, the card shows individual pills but no summary count. Seeing "3 / 5 filters passing" at a glance tells the trader whether a signal is close or far.
Add a compact X / 5 counter in the signal title line — colour-coded: grey=0-2, amber=3-4, green=5 (signal). Very cheap to compute (already have the pills).
MEDLIVE Today's running P&L tally at the top
The signal log exists but is buried below the charts. During live trading the most important number is "how am I doing today?" — yet you have to scroll past two full charts to see it.
Add a compact 3-metric row at the very top of the live tab (before the cards): Today's Trades, Today's Points, Open P&L — read from the signal log CSV. Always visible without scrolling.
MEDLIVE Dead zone / skip reason visibility on the signal card
When the signal engine returns NONE because of a time filter (BNKN 10:00-11:30, NIFTY BUY 11:30-12:30, expiry day), the reason string shows it — but it's buried at the bottom of the card in small grey text. A trader might misread this as "no signal conditions met" rather than "blocked by time rule".
When reason starts with "BNKN dead zone", "NIFTY BUY dead zone", or "Expiry day" — show an amber banner at the top of the card explaining the block clearly: "⏰ BNKN Dead Zone (10:00–11:30) — next entry window: 11:30". Much more actionable.
LOWLIVE Countdown to next candle close
On a 5-minute chart, knowing you are 4 minutes 20 seconds from the next completed candle tells you whether to watch closely or step away. Currently no timing context at all.
Show "Next candle in: 3m 12s" based on the wall clock aligned to 5-minute boundaries. Pure client-side time math, no API call, but genuinely useful during live monitoring.
LOWLIVE Chart: Volume profile if available (or candle range width)
Index candles have zero volume, but candle range (H-L width) is a valid volatility proxy. Wide candles = high momentum; narrow doji candles = indecision. Adding an optional 4th panel with candle range (or volume for stocks) would help confirm entry quality.
Add optional 4th row to the chart showing H-L range per candle as a bar chart. Gate it behind a checkbox to not make the chart unwieldy by default.

TAB 2 — Backtest

Current state: Good stats, equity curve, daily P&L, exit reason table, trade log in expander. The gaps are analysis depth — you can see what happened but not easily why or when it worked.
HIGHBACKTEST Per-hour-slot heatmap (Win Rate % by entry time)
The dead zones (10:00-11:30 BNKN, 11:30-12:30 NIFTY BUY) were discovered by analysing exactly this data. Having a live slot heatmap in the UI means you can visually validate whether the current backtest confirms those rules — or whether a different slot is now the weak link for the next two weeks.
Group trades by entry_time (floored to 30-min slot). Show a table: slot | trades | W | L | WR% | Points — with cell colouring. Uses existing trade data, zero extra computation.
HIGHBACKTEST Hold-time distribution (Win vs Loss hold time comparison)
The backtester comment says "BUY wins avg hold = 188 min; BUY losses exited within avg 50 min". This insight drove the ST confirmation rule. Currently this metric is completely invisible in the UI. Seeing it on every run validates whether that pattern still holds.
Compute average hold duration for WIN trades vs LOSS trades (from entry_time to exit_time). Show as two metric boxes side by side: "Avg Win Hold" and "Avg Loss Hold". If loss hold ≪ win hold = fast-reversal losses (false flip). If similar = trend failure losses (different problem).
MEDBACKTEST Weekly performance table
The daily P&L bar chart shows noise. Zooming out to weekly totals reveals whether the strategy has consistent positive weeks or relies on one or two lucky days. Over 2-4 weeks of new data this becomes the most important view.
Add a weekly P&L table alongside the daily chart: group by ISO week → sum points, count trades, WR%. 5 rows for a 30-day run, 12 rows for 90 days. Immediately shows if there's a weak week pattern.
MEDBACKTEST RSI entry distribution chart
For the next two weeks of validation you want to confirm whether the RSI band tuning is holding. A simple histogram of RSI at entry for WIN vs LOSS trades shows immediately if losses are clustering at the edges of the allowed band.
Using existing rsi column in trade log, bucket into 5-point bins and show WIN/LOSS counts side by side. A plain bar chart — no new data needed.
MEDBACKTEST Drawdown curve overlaid on equity curve
The equity curve shows cumulative points but not when the drawdowns occurred. The max drawdown stat is a single number — it hides whether it was one catastrophic streak or steady grinding losses. Overlaying the underwater curve makes the risk profile much clearer.
In the equity curve chart, add a second trace (right y-axis or same panel) showing rolling drawdown from peak. Already computable from points.cumsum().
LOWBACKTEST Trade duration histogram
Knowing whether this is a "quick 15-minute trade" strategy or a "hold for 2 hours" strategy affects how you monitor it. A simple histogram of hold durations (in 15-min buckets) gives this picture in one glance.
Compute hold time per trade from entry/exit times, bucket into 15-min slots, show bar chart. Small addition alongside existing charts.

TAB 3 — Journal

Current state: Good weekly review tool. The gap is the absence of trend-over-time views — you can see total performance but not whether it's improving week on week.
HIGHJOURNAL Rolling 5-day / 10-day Win Rate trend line
For the next two weeks of validation, the most important question is "is WR stable, improving, or degrading?" A single number doesn't show this. A rolling WR line chart (5-trade or 10-trade rolling window) immediately visualises regime changes.
Compute rolling WR% (binary win=1/loss=0 rolling mean over last N trades × 100). Plot as a line chart alongside the equity curve. Critical for spotting if the tuned parameters are decaying.
MEDJOURNAL Month-over-month comparison table
Once 4-6 weeks of data accumulates the Journal needs a month breakdown: Jul vs Aug etc. Currently the only groupings are daily and "all time". This is the natural review cadence for any systematic trading approach.
Group by date.strftime("%Y-%m") → show: month | trades | WR% | total pts | avg/trade. A simple aggregation of existing data.
MEDJOURNAL Streak history timeline (not just max)
The current "Max Consec Loss" stat is one number. But knowing when the streaks happened (e.g. 3 consecutive losses on 3 consecutive Thursdays) reveals the expiry day pattern. The full streak timeline is much more informative.
Build a compact table: each streak of 2+ consecutive losses, its start date, length, and total points lost. Small loop over the existing result column.
