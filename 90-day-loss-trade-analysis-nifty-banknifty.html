Key insight: SL is being hit at full-cap width on almost every loss
In the worst-10 trade list, 8 of 10 Nifty losses show initial_risk = 55.0 pts — exactly the hard cap. This means the Supertrend SL was already wider than the cap at entry, so the effective SL was the cap itself from the very first candle. These are "cap-distance entries" — the trade was at max risk from the moment it opened, with no cushion from Supertrend.
Not a flaw in logic — the cap is doing its job as backstop. But it signals entries taken when Supertrend is unusually wide relative to recent ATR.
EOD losses — 7 Nifty / 6 BankNifty trades forced closed at 15:15 at a loss
These are trades that were entered in the right direction but didn't recover before session end. They represent 15.5% (Nifty) and 9.4% (BankNifty) of total loss points. Average loss on EOD exits: −56 pts (Nifty), −110 pts (BankNifty). The two worst EOD losses (Nifty −132 pts, BankNifty −301 pts) were entered after 11:00 AM and never recovered.
Candidate for improvement: tighter "no new entry after 11:30" rule for late-day reversal setups.

Losing trades die in under an hour — wins run for ~3 hours. This is healthy asymmetry.
The Supertrend trail is working exactly as designed: wins ride the trend for hours while stops cut losses quickly. The median loss exits at 50–60 min, the median win holds for 165–168 min — a 3:1 time asymmetry that complements the 1.64–1.68 points R:R.

Nifty loss bucket breakdown: losses are spread almost evenly across all hold durations (0–15m: 9 trades, 15–30m: 10, 30–60m: 12, 1–2h: 13, 2h+: 9). This suggests no single "quick false-signal" problem — losses happen at all holding times, typical of trend-following.

No change recommended here — this pattern is intrinsic to Supertrend trailing. Cutting losses faster would also cut wins that temporarily dip before recovering.

Nifty median win is only +1.00R — but mean is +9.22R due to large outlier runs
The median win captures exactly 1R (Supertrend SL holds just one bar then flips). But Nifty's mean win of +9.22R shows that a subset of trades ride enormous moves (the max win was +258R — a 300+ pt trade on a 1-pt initial risk trade). This right-skewed distribution is the hallmark of a good trend-following system: most wins are modest but occasional huge wins carry the P&L.
BankNifty losses average −2.28R — losses are exceeding 1R
The mean loss R of −2.28 (vs median −1.00) means some losses are significantly larger than the initial risk. The worst BankNifty loss was −64.73R. This happens when price gaps through the SL or when ATR expands rapidly after entry (so the dynamic cap widens before it can protect).
The dynamic ATR cap is re-evaluated every candle, but a sharp volatility spike between candles can cause slippage beyond the cap. This is an inherent limitation of 5-min candle granularity. No simple fix without moving to tick data.

What to Leave Alone

  • SL Hit as primary exit mechanism — 78–89% of loss points come here, but this is by design. Any attempt to widen the SL to "avoid" hits will simply increase loss magnitude. The SL is already optimally tuned.
  • Avg hold duration of losses (68–73 min) — Losses exit quickly. This is ideal. Do not add a time-stop — it would cut profitable slow-developing trades.
  • RSI exit (BUY RSI<40 / SELL RSI>60) — Contributes only 5–6% of losses and the exits it generates are mostly correct momentum failures. Tightening would create more false exits.
  • The 12:00–13:00 window — 77.3% / 71.4% WR, best of all phases. Never restrict this window.
  • BankNifty overall — Despite 50 losses, it generates +7,055 pts in 90 days. It is working well. The improvements above are optimizations, not fixes.
