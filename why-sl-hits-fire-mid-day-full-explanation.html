Why Do SL Hits Fire Mid-Day?

90-day backtest · 80 total SL Hits (NIFTY 40 + BANKNIFTY 40) · All classified by root cause

Short answer: Every SL Hit is one of exactly three things. None of them are bugs. The mid-day SL hits you are seeing are Type C — the Supertrend initial band was very close to the entry price (10–40 pts), so a normal intraday retracement of just that many points triggers it before the trend can resume.

A Hard-Cap Hits — "The market moved against me immediately"

What you see: Exit reason = SL Hit, points = exactly −45.0 (NIFTY) or a large number (BNKN −60 to −191).
Why it happens: A strong move (news, opening gap, momentum reversal) pushes price −45 pts (NIFTY) or 2×ATR (BNKN) in one or two candles. The Supertrend trailing stop has no time to adjust — it fires at the initial hard cap.
Example: 2026-05-13 SELL 09:40 → exit 09:50, −45 pts in 10 minutes. Opening gap reversed sharply upward.

B Trail Wins — "The trade worked, then reversed"

What you see: Exit reason = SL Hit, but points are positive (+3 to +389).
Why it happens: The trade moved in-favour for 1–4 hours. The Supertrend line trailed upward (BUY) / downward (SELL), raising the SL above entry. When price eventually reversed, it hit the trailed SL, not the original one — locking in profit.
These are exactly what the trailing SL is designed to do. The trade reached the 15:00 threshold on other days — on these days the trend reversed before 15:00 but not before the ST could trail to profit.

C Near-Entry Reversals — "The specific trades you asked about"

What you see: SL Hit mid-day, loss of −1 to −44 pts. Not the full cap. Confusing because the day isn't over.
Root cause — the Supertrend band gap: On the entry candle, the Supertrend line (which becomes the initial SL) is set by the formula HL/2 ± 3×ATR. When ATR is low (calm market) or the candle is a strong push, the ST band can be very close to the entry price — sometimes only 15–25 pts away. Any normal intraday pullback of that size hits the SL.

The mechanics — why ST gap varies

Supertrend SL formula: SL = (High + Low)/2 ± 3 × ATR(14)

When ATR is low (e.g. 6–8 pts on a quiet day) the ST band is only 18–24 pts from mid-price.
When ATR is high (e.g. 15 pts on a volatile day) the ST band is 45 pts away → hits the hard cap first.

The "tight ST" problem happens specifically on low-volatility afternoons where RSI drifts into the 53–57 BUY zone on thin volume. The Supertrend flips GREEN but the band is only 15–25 pts below close. Any minor intraday retracement hits it.

Summary — Why Mid-Day SL Hits Are Normal

QuestionAnswer
Why do some SL hits give −20 pts, not −45?The Supertrend band was only 20 pts from entry. The initial SL = ST band, which moves with volatility.
Why does the SL fire in the middle of the day (e.g. 13:05)?Price retraced back to the ST line, which is always below entry (BUY) or above entry (SELL). When price reaches it, SL fires immediately regardless of time.
Why doesn't the SL trail away before the reversal?The Supertrend only trails when price moves further in-favour. If price chops sideways or dips slightly, the ST line stays at entry level. A 20-pt retracement hits the still-near-entry SL.
Is this a bug or error?No. It is correct behaviour. The SL is the ST line = the exact point where the trend is considered reversed.
Are the 15:00 EOD trades compensating?Yes. EOD exits = +1,910 pts NIFTY (95% WR). The 16 Type-C losses = −423 pts total. The EOD edge is 4.5× larger than all Type-C losses combined.
Could we reduce Type-C losses?Only by raising the minimum ST width at entry (e.g. skip entry if ST gap < 25 pts). Worth testing — affects ~16 NIFTY trades, could save ~−200 pts. But risks removing some entries that go to EOD.
The big picture: All 80 SL hits across both instruments generated a net of −3,196 pts combined. The 42 EOD exits generated +7,092 pts. The SL hits are the entry fee you pay to hold positions through to 15:00. The strategy's edge comes entirely from surviving to EOD — not from avoiding SL hits.
